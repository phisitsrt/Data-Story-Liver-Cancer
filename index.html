Alcohol is not the main and only reason causes Liver cancer.

by Phisit Srirattanawong

For a decade, many people said that alcohol is the main reason that cause the liver cancer. There are a lot of campaigns out there which aim to decrease the alcohol consumption to prevent the liver cancer. However, if we look closely to statistic like the alcohol consumption per capitar and the crude rate of liver cancer as shown in the bar chart above. It seems to be not a strong relationship between them. Belarus is the country with the highest alcohol consume rate at 16.34 litre in 2012. Even though, the crude rate is just only 3.40% in the same year. Comparing to France, this country is in the seventh rank in the alcohol consumption list but in the top of the crude rate list. In addition, France is the only country which in the top 10 of both list. Even we look only on each type of beverage namely beer, wine, spirit and other alcoholic beverages, the crude rate still not rank in the same way with consumption rate as well. According to the Cancer Research UK, there are many components that cause the liver cancer including alcohol consumption, smoking, and overweight issue.

Smoking, another cause of liver cancer

Comparing the smoking rate in adult with liver cancer crude rate, it shows that the crude rate trend to be higher in the country which have more percentage of smoking adult. However, smoking ratio for both male and female in France is not outstanding from other country. It still on the average of the world. But why the crude rate of liver cancer in this country is very high? We might assume that not only alcohol consumption and smoking causing the cancer but combining with other factors together.

Male, higher chance of getting a liver cancer.

Now, we look closely on independent gender. The risk of having cancer in male is higher than female on every indicator. These risks are double in men which is a very large number. It consistent with the research from American Cancer Society and Cancer Research UK. They have given the reason on this result that it becauses of a life style of men trend to drink and smoke more than women as well as other factors that will rise the risk of cancer such as overweight and obesity.

Overweight, another factor to be in concern.

Not many people notice that this will make them risky for the liver cancer. Overweight chart illustrates that the country in the top list of the liver cancer crude rate mostly have higher overweight male citizen than female. But the percentage of overweight adult in these group have only 3 countries that have over weight citizen more than the world average at 49%. Above all, we can see that one factor alone might not make you have higher chance to have liver cancer. However, when combining these behavior together will rise the risk as seen from the gender chart.


What should we do?

In order to prevent the liver cancer, reducing alcoholic beverage consumption, lose weight and stop smoking are the easiest way for us to control. Even if there are other factors like genetic that we cannot control them, this way is still the recommended way to avoid liver cancer. For the government, they should inform people of all the risk factors not just focus only on alcohol.
